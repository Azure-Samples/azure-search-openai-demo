Greenhouse Gas Research CentreFind out about the New Zealand Agricultural Greenhouse Gas Research Centre, the work it does, and MPI's involvement.UPDATE – 11 JUNE 2024The Government is increasing funding for the centreThe New Zealand Agricultural Greenhouse Gas Research Centre (NZAGRC) plays an important role in identifying and funding critical research and development.An additional $50.5 million will be spent over the next 5 years to support the development of new emissions reduction tools.Government scales up funding for NZAGRC – Beehive Helping agriculture reduce greenhouse gas emissionsThe New Zealand Agricultural Greenhouse Gas Research Centre (NZAGRC) is focused on delivering fit-for-purpose emissions reduction tools and technologies to ensure thriving farmers and growers, and a healthy environment.The NZAGRC was established in 2010 and is hosted by AgResearch, with its headquarters in Palmerston North.The NZAGRC has been shaping and managing research programmes that have taken mitigation research forward globally.Its networks and knowledge of the current state of mitigation research in New Zealand, and internationally, helps shape future research.It funds a wide range of research providers to deliver programmes, often co-funded with others.Find out more about NZAGRC Centre for Climate Action on Agricultural EmissionsThe New Zealand Agricultural Greenhouse Gas Research Centre (NZAGRC) is a key component of the Centre for Climate Action on Agricultural Emissions (CCAAE).It plays an important role in identifying and funding critical research and development, alongside AgriZeroNZ’s investment efforts in commercially promising technologies, some of which have their genesis in the NZAGRC.Find out more about AgriZeroNZ NZAGRC investmentAdditional funding of $50.5 million over 5 years from MPI to scale up the NZAGRC.A total investment of $149.6 million over 5 years from MPI into the NZAGRC’s domestic research and development. Extra investment in agricultural emissions reduction research 2024 – Fact sheet  [PDF, 2.3 MB]Research focusesNZAGRC is investing in New Zealand agricultural greenhouse gas emissions research. Its vision is to be an internationally renowned centre for research and development.The centre's research mainly focuses on:ruminant methanenitrous oxidesoil carbonintegrated farm systems, andidentifying and advancing Mātauranga and Te Ao Māori approaches to biological emissions reductions.It also aims to:improve coordination of greenhouse gas research and research infrastructurebuild New Zealand's research capacity by supporting students, early career scientists, technicians, and senior scientists.Involvement with the Global Research AllianceNZAGRC plays a major role in the Global Research Alliance on Agricultural Greenhouse Gases (GRA).
