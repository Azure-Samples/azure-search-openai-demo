Costs GrantTenancy Costs CoverTransition to Alternative Housing GrantTransfer to another public housing propertyOther languagesTenancy Costs CoverIf you're trying to get a rental property but feel you're being turned down due to your personal situation, we may be able to help. Your personal situation might be that you:have a bad credit historyhave a criminal recordhave a language barrier, orsomething else. We can assure landlords that we'll help to pay for any costs you may have at the end of your tenancy if it ends within 2 years. These are outstanding costs over and above your bond amount. It could cover things like property damage or rent you owe. This is called 'Tenancy Costs Cover'.Apply while you're looking for a rental property. We'll give you a letter with this information that you can show to landlords. This is called a 'Letter of assurance'. If you get accepted into a rental property, your landlord needs to sign the Letter of assurance. Then if you have costs to cover at the end of your tenancy, you can lodge a claim to get Tenancy Costs Cover paid. If you don't agree to the costs, your landlord can make a claim.Who can get itYou may be able to get Tenancy Costs Cover if you have taken reasonable steps to find a rental property. For example, you have:applied for rental propertiesgone to rental property viewingsregistered with a rental agency or housing brokerobtained character references if necessary. You also need to be entering a tenancy that:the Residential Tenancies Act (RTA) applies to, i.e. you'll have a tenancy agreement, andrequires you to pay a bond of 4 weeks rent. You must find a rental property within 6 months of getting a Letter of assurance.We'll also take into account other factors, such as:your situationif you can afford to stay in the accommodation long-termhow long you’ll be living in your accommodation (generally this needs to be at least 28 days)if this is the best payment for youwhat other help is available to you. There is also residency criteria you need to meet.Residency criteriaYou must be either:getting a main benefit, NZ Super or Veteran's Pension, orordinarily resident in New Zealand and either: a New Zealand citizenhave a residence class visa under the Immigration Act 2009recognised as a refugee or protected person under the Immigration Act 2009, orgetting a Special Needs Grant because you came to New Zealand on a temporary visa to be with your New Zealand partner and your relationship ended due to domestic violence.   What you can getWe'll give you a letter that explains:what Tenancy Costs Cover is, andwhat costs it could help pay for at the end of your tenancy. You can give this letter to landlords while you're looking for a house.The most you can get to cover outstanding costs at the end of your tenancy is a payment equal to 4 weeks rent. Your tenancy will need to have ended within 2 years and you’ll need to pay this money back.Outstanding costs are costs over and above your bond amount, and could cover things like:property damagerent you owe. How to applyYou need to apply while you're looking for a house.Step 1 - Call usCall us on 0800 559 009If you get NZ Super or Veteran's Pension, call Senior Services on 0800 552 002Step 2 - Go to an appointmentWe'll book you an appointment to come in and see us.At your appointment, we'll check if you qualify for Tenancy Costs Cover and give you an application form to fill out.Step 3 - Get a Letter of assuranceIf your application's approved, we'll give you a letter of assurance to give to landlords while you're looking for a house. This will explain what your tenancy costs cover is and what costs it could help pay for at the end of your tenancy.The letter is valid for 6 months. If you don't find a house within 6 months, the letter will expire. We may be able to issue you a new letter – call us on 0800 559 009 to talk about this.Once you find a houseIf you get accepted into a rental property, you need to get your landlord to sign the Letter of assurance.You also need to gather these documents:bond receipt from the landlordtenancy agreement. You can drop the letter and documents off to your local Work and Income service centre or post them to us.We’ll also send a copy of the letter to your landlord for their records.Find your nearest service centreAt the end of your tenancyIf your tenancy ends within 2 years and you have costs that your bond won’t cover, you can lodge a claim. If you stay in the house for longer than 2 years, you won’t be able to claim.The claim needs to be lodged within 3 months after your tenancy ends. If you don't agree to the costs, the landlord can make a claim. They'll need to provide a Tenancy Tribunal or court order confirming you owe them accommodation costs.How to make a claimYou can lodge a claim by showing us one of these documents:Tenancy agreementMediator’s Order, orTenancy Tribunal Order form. You can bring this document into your local service centre or post it to us.Find your nearest service centreIf the claim is approvedIf the claim is approved, we'll pay the money directly to your landlord.We'll check if your landlord's already a supplier with us. If they're not, we'll ask them to register as a supplier by completing a 'Supplier Registration Form'.
