expensesYou're not workingRelationshipsSeniorsUrgent costsMoving to NZHaving a babyWe may be able to help during your pregnancy and after your baby is born.PregnantInformationGo online with SmartStart and get step-by-step information and support to help you access the right services for you and your baby: SmartStart website.If you're pregnant, it's important that you get a midwife and access maternity services. Talk to your doctor, or the local hospital maternity services, to find out what's available in your area.Getting a benefitHaving a baby while you're on a benefitIf you're pregnant and getting a benefit, you may have a change in obligations.On a low income Check what you might getUnable to workJobseeker SupportYou may be able to get Jobseeker Support if your health practitioner (eg, your midwife or doctor) says you're unable to work because of your pregnancy. You'll need a medical certificate from your health practitioner.After the baby is bornGetting a benefitHaving a baby while you're on a benefitIf you're having a baby and getting a benefit, you may have a change in obligations or your payments could be affected.There are also other types of support that you may be able to get.Working for FamiliesBest Start tax credit (BSTC) is a payment to help families with costs in a child's first 3 years.If you're getting a Work and Income benefit, we'll pay you the Best Start tax credit with your payments.You'll need to apply through Inland Revenue (IR) if you don't get a benefit from Work and Income.It is also available to caregivers getting either:Orphan's BenefitUnsupported Child's Benefit, orFoster Care Allowance. Advance Payment of BenefitIf you're getting a benefit, we may be able to help with an advance payment.Recoverable Assistance PaymentIf you're not getting a benefit, we may be able to help with a recoverable payment.Breast pumpsSpecial Needs GrantHelp with a breast pump if you can't breastfeed because you or your baby have health issues.Finding or starting work – help with costsHelp with a breast pump if you can't breastfeed because you're returning to work.Disability AllowanceHelp with a breast pump if you or your baby have an ongoing disability.ChildcareChildcareWe may be able to help with childcare costs, depending on your circumstances.On your own with one or more childrenSole Parent SupportYou might be able to get Sole Parent Support, a payment that will help you get ready for future work and supports you to find part or full-time work.Teen parentYoung Parent PaymentIf you're 16-19, you might be able to get a Young Parent Payment from us.Multiple birth - need helpHome HelpWe may be able to help you with the cost of home help when you've had a multiple birth.Your baby is sick and needs ongoing medical careCaring for a child with a health condition or disabilityIf you need additional help with medical expenses we may be able to help.RelatedGovernment services available when you're having a childPaid Parental leaveParental Tax CreditWorking for Families Tax CreditsChild SupportHelp with housing costsCommunity Services CardContactIf you're unsure about the help you can get contact usHow we can helpBenefits and paymentsWorkOn a benefitHousingOnline servicesCheck what you might getFind workApply onlineMyMSDHow to stay safe onlineAboutAbout Work and IncomeAbout this siteAccessibilityPrivacy, disclaimer and copyrightContact usPhone numbers                    Find a service centre                    Facebook                    Twitter                    Feedback                              Help in other languages        Te Kāwanatanga o Aotearoa /
