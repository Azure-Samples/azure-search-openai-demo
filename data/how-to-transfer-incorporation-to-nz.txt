This means your company will then become a New Zealand company and you must give up the registration in the country where your company is incorporated.Before you apply, the company must have at least 1 director who lives in:New Zealand, orAustralia and is a director of a company incorporated in Australia.                                                                                 Who can be a                                            director                                Note        After the transfer has taken place, any constitutional documents you operated under in the country where your company was originally incorporated will no longer be valid.    How to applyThe application process involves several steps.1. Reserve your company nameBefore you start your application, you need to set up an online services account so you can reserve your company name.                                                                                 Setting up your online services                                            account                                The name you reserve must end in Limited, if the shareholders are to have limited liability. There is a fee of $10 (plus GST) for this service.                                                                                 How to reserve a company                                            name                                2. Apply to register your companyDownload and complete Form 20. It must be signed by a director of the company.                                         Form 20 — Application by overseas company to register as a company                PDF, 103 KB opens in a new windowWith your signed and completed form, you need to send us:confirmation of your name reservation — this is emailed to you after you've reserved it onlineyour Certificate of Incorporation, or an equivalent document, from the country where the company is currently incorporateda certified copy of your company's constitutiona statutory declaration from a director or another authorised person confirming that there is nothing stopping your company from incorporating in New Zealand.If any of the documents are not in English        You must provide a certified translation.    If your company is already on our Overseas RegisterIf your company is already registered — for example, if it's been operating as a branch in New Zealand — and you've already submitted your incorporation and constitutional documents to us, you don't need to provide them again.Making a statutory declarationYour declaration must comply with the Oaths and Declarations Act 1957. How you make the declaration depends on whether you make it in New Zealand or in another country.                                                             Oaths and Declarations Act 1957 — Parts 9 to 11                                                                New Zealand Legislation                                        3. Submit your applicationSend your completed form and documents to:New Zealand Companies Office National Processing Centre Private Bag 92061 Victoria Street West Auckland 1142 New Zealand4. How we process your applicationWe review your documents and if they're in order we'll advise you to complete the transfer by registering your company using our online services. We'll attach your completed Form 20 and the accompanying documents to your online application.It costs $118.74 (plus GST) to incorporate a company.5.
