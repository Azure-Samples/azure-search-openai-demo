wellbeing fund for rural communitiesRural proofing: guidance for policymakersWood Processing Growth FundJobs for NatureOn Farm SupportLast reviewed: 15.08.24Wood Processing Growth FundThe Wood Processing Growth Fund (WPGF) aims to expand New Zealand's wood processing sector. It will help unlock the potential of our forestry resource by providing investment support. Find out more about the fund and how to apply.About the fundThe Wood Processing Growth Fund is designed to help wood processors increase New Zealand's onshore wood processing capacity. It does this by providing both grants for pre-investment activities, and direct investment (debt or equity) in capital projects.The fund focuses on high value, long-lived wood products like sawn structural timber and engineered wood, for domestic and export markets.Examples of long-lived wood products include:structural framing timberfurnituredeckingmouldingsfencinglandscaping and retaining walls.What the fund is not forIt does not fund processes like wood fuels, bio-char, and single-use products like packaging, or other bio-materials and bio-products.Biofuel and energy transition projects may access support through the Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority (EECA).Co-funding and support - EECA Biomaterials and other innovative projects using bio-based feedstocks may access support through Sustainable Food and Fibre Futures.Sustainable Food and Fibre FuturesInfrastructure projects may access support through the Regional Infrastructure Fund.Regional Infrastructure Fund - Grow Regions Three funds are availableThe Wood Processing Growth Fund supports wood processors by co-investing in their projects. The 3 funds are:Catalyst – grants to support pre-investment activities for wood processing projectsTe Kōtuku – grants to help grow small-scale processors of non-pine timberAccelerator – debt or equity investment in capital projects to expand wood processing.If you're not sure which category your project fits into, let us know. We will work with you to find the best approach.Expand allCatalystFind out if you're eligible and how to apply.Toggle expandableStatus: Open. Contact us now to check your eligibility to apply.Funding pool available: Up to $2 million a year. About Catalyst grantsCatalyst grants help with the costs of pre-investment activities for wood processing projects. If your project is at the pre-investment stage, you can apply for a Catalyst grant to develop it further.Catalyst grants can co-fund:business cases and feasibility studiesresearch and pilotstechnical and business development assistanceentrepreneurial capability building, to help groups and investors develop commercial capability and skillsskills development associated with improving productivitymarket development projects.Catalyst provides grants for typically up to half the total cost of these activities.If you get a Catalyst grant, the results of the activities you get funding for can be used to support your application for an Accelerator loan. For example, results from research or a feasibility study.Who can applyTo be eligible to apply, your project should:aim to increase wood processing of long-lived wood productsbe a New Zealand-based wood processing projectbe supported by co-funding from non-government investors.For Catalyst grants, we invite you to get in touch if your project is at the pre-investment stage.We’ll assess applications to make sure the projects:align with government priorities to boost wood processing of long-lived wood productsare well-planned and backed by specialist expertisecan increase the processing of long-lived wood products at the firm or sector level.How to applyGet in touch with us to discuss your project and the application process:email wpgf@mpi.govt.nzcontact our freephone call centre on: 0800 00 83 33.Catalyst grant recipients BiotanexBiotanex is a New Zealand-owned timber manufacturing business based in Napier. It uses water-based treatments to enhance the weight, durability, and performance of timber. Biotanex’s products are suitable for both interior uses like panels and flooring, and exterior uses like decking and cladding. The business will use the grant to fund testing to meet national and international building standards, and secure IP rights. This will help Biotanex expand its market presence and commercialise its products.Biotanex received a $258,479 grant.Abodo WoodAbodo Wood sells long-lived building material wood products, including Vulcan Cladding. The cladding is New Zealand's first verified carbon-negative feature timber. The grant will allow Abodo Wood to bring part of the manufacturing process for the cladding in-house. This will double the company’s production capacity for the cladding.Abodo Wood received a $125,000 grant.Summit ForestsSummit Forests started its New Zealand operations in 2013. It’s now the seventh largest forestry business in New Zealand with over 50,000 hectares (ha) of forestry. This grant will allow Summit Forests to develop a pre-feasibility study for a wood processing facility in Tairāwhiti, including the option of a mill at Ruatōria. The study will explore options for the facility’s processing type, size, location, feedstocks, and products.Summit Forests received a $163,500 grant.Tumu TimbersTumu Timbers is a timber manufacturing business based in Hastings, Hawke's Bay. The grant will allow Tumu Timbers to investigate the viability of producing and selling kitset-style agricultural buildings. The buildings will be made mostly of Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL). They’ll use cost-effective connections and efficient assembly methods.Tumu Timbers received a $83,375 grant.WoodspanWoodspan is a New Zealand owned manufacturer and fabricator of Parallel Laminated Timber (PLT) mass timber panels. They also make glulam engineered wood products. The grant allows Woodspan to look into producing a mass-timber PLT ground floor system. This system would be for residential use. It could offer a cheaper alternative to traditional concrete or timber subfloor systems.Woodspan received a $87,500 grant.Te KōtukuFind out if you're eligible and how to apply.Toggle expandableStatus: Closed to applications.Funding pool available: Up to $500,000 a year. Each grant is a minimum of $50,000. Funding for the 2024-2025 financial year has been allocated. Contact us to check your eligibility to apply for funding from July 2025.About Te Kōtuku grantsTe Kōtuku fund provides grants to small-scale processors that specialise in using non-pine trees to lift productivity and capacity. The grants can be used to fund plant and equipment (capital projects).Applicants are expected to contribute around 50% co-funding towards the project cost. There is some flexibility where the application can demonstrate significant benefit.Te Kōtuku can fund a range of investment which lifts productivity and capacity of operations that process less than 15,000 cubic metres a year.Te Kōtuku can fund investments for:inflow equipment for example, loaders, telehandlers, rough terrain forklifts, log decksprimary processing equipment for example, portable millssecondary processing equipment for example, planers, moulders, dust extraction upgrades, resaws, thicknessersupgrades to kilns and boilers for example, dehumidifying kilns or solar kilnsenergy infrastructure for example, waste chippers, boilerstreatment equipment for example, chemical, thermalother improvements, new equipment or upgrades.Who can applyTo be eligible for Te Kōtuku grants, your project must:be based in Aotearoa New Zealandprocess less than 15,000 cubic metres log input (or equivalent) annuallywant to scale up or improve processing of non-Pinus radiata tree species (alternative exotic species and/or legally harvested native trees) into high-value, long-lived wood productshave an annual processing capacity of a minimum of 1,000 cubic metres within 3 years of project completionhave a detailed plan to show how your business plans to grow and expand in the next 5 years.How to applyGet in touch with us to discuss your project and the application process:email wpgf@mpi.govt.nzcontact our freephone call centre on: 0800 00 83 33.Te Kōtuku grant recipientsBennetts SawmillBennetts Sawmill is a small-scale mill. They supply Oregon, Larch, and Macrocarpa timber across Canterbury. The mill’s new owners saw a market need for reliable, high-quality, non-pine timber. The grant will fund new equipment, increasing production from 600 m³ to 10,000 m³ annually. This will enhance efficiency and sustainability. It will also support the fencing, railing, outdoor landscaping, and construction industries.Bennetts Sawmill received a $280,534 grant.AcceleratorThe second investment round closes on 26 August 2024. Toggle expandableStatus: Open. Contact us now to check your eligibility to apply. Applications must be received before 26 August 2024.Funding pool available: $33 million.About AcceleratorAccelerator provides debt and/or equity finance for up to 50% of the total cost of capital activities that increase onshore processing capacity of long-lived wood products like sawn structural timber and engineered wood. Applicants will need to source finance for the remainder of the costs.Accelerator can co-fund investments including:equipmenttechnologybuildingsinfrastructure.It cannot be used to buy land.There is $33 million available in total for capital co-investments, alongside private capital. As the proceeds of investments are returned, they will be available for reinvestment in future investment rounds.Who can apply?To be eligible to apply, your project should be:a New Zealand-based capital projectlikely to stimulate more onshore wood processing of long-lived wood productssupported by co-funding from non-government investors.For the Accelerator, we invite you to apply if you are investment ready. This means you will:have already conducted your due diligenceknow your investment is both technically and commercially feasibleuse existing technlogy that can offer early returns on investmenthave the necessary documentation, including a detailed business case.We’ll assess applications to make sure the projects:increase output of long-lived wood productsshow they’re well-advanced and will provide returns on investmentare well planned and backed by specialist expertiseinclude private finance.How to applyGet in touch with us to discuss your project and the application process.We might encourage you to consider applying for a Catalyst grant if your project needs more development.To contact us:email wpgf@mpi.govt.nzcontact our freephone call centre on: 0800 00 83 33.Accelerator recipientsRosvall SawmillRosvall Sawmill is a modern timber processing sawmill based in Whangārei. They make timber products for residential and industrial construction. The loan will co-finance a state of the art treatment facility. This will enable them to scale-up processing by 42,000 cubic metres of logs per year. It will also create 8 full time jobs on site and facilitate considerable total growth for the period ahead.Rosvall Sawmill received a $2 million loan.Who to contactIf you have any questions:email wpgf@mpi.govt.nzcontact our freephone call centre on: 0800 00 83 33.Or write to:Wood Processing Growth Fund Team  Te Uru Rākau – New Zealand Forest Service  PO Box 2526  Wellington 6140.FeedbackContact usSubscribe to MPIScienceLegalResources and formsMPI website disclaimerSitemapCopyrightPrivacy and securityMPI business unitsMPI uses cookies to improve your experience on our site.
