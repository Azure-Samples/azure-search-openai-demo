Debt repayment informationIf you're getting a main benefit, NZ Super, Veteran's Pension or Accommodation Supplement, there's a different process for your debt repayments.What we mean by 'main benefit'Repaying debt while getting a benefit or pension from usHelp with your debtIf you need help with your debt or want to talk about it, you can contact our Client Support Debt Management teamDebt repayment amountBelow is a guide of how much you could repay. This is just a guide, it depends how much you can afford.Debt amount Repayment amount  Less than $2,000$25 a week ($1,300 a year) $2,000 - $3,500$30 a week ($1,560 a year) $3,500 - $5,000$35 a week ($1,820 a year) $5,000 - $6,000$40 a week ($2,080 a year) $6,000 - $8,000$45 a week ($2,340 a year) More than $8,000$50 a week ($2,600 a year)    When deciding how much your repayments should be, you'll need to think of things like:your budgetyour upcoming expensesany other debts you may havehow long you want to be repaying the money. Talk to us about your repayment amountYou need to contact our Client Support Debt Management team about your repayment amount. They'll work with you on a repayment plan based on what you can afford.When you call, they'll ask you:your contact details, e.g. address, phone and emailabout your incomeabout your expensesif you have any dependent children. You may be asked to provide proof.Setting up debt repaymentsWe want to help you repay your debt in a way that best suits your situation.We have a range of repayment options available, it depends if you're making payments from New Zealand or overseas.Repay debt from New ZealandRepay debt from overseasWhile repaying your debt - tell us about any changesIf you have a change in circumstances while repaying your debt, you need to contact our Client Support Debt Management teamThis could be changes for:your addressyour contact detailsif you go overseas. If your debt repayments are being deducted from your wagesIf you get a new job, let our Client Support Debt Management team know before you start.Then they can arrange for repayments to be deducted from your wages with your new employer.If you miss a debt repaymentIf you miss a repayment, we'll send you a reminder letter. We don't charge penalties or interest.If you're having trouble repaying your debt, contact our Client Support Debt Management team. They can work with you on a repayment plan that suits you.If you don't repay your debtWe understand that it can be hard to stretch your budget to include debt repayments.If you're struggling to repay your debt, contact our Client Support Debt Management team. They can work with you on a repayment plan based on what you can afford.We won't charge interest or impose penalties on your debt.What happens if you don't repay your debtWe'll try to contact you several times about your debt. If you don't respond, we'll seek to get the money back in other ways.To find out your current circumstances, we compare information with other government agencies, e.g. Inland Revenue.There are legal steps we can take which allow us to recover your debt without your consent, such as:making deductions from your wages, ormaking deductions from your bank account.
